About Me

I am an MPhil graduate in Public Relations and Advertising from the University of the Punjab, with a published research paper in the Consumer Behavior Review — an international peer-reviewed journal.

My academic interests centre on the intersection of social media, digital activism, cultural behaviour, and generational identity in South Asia. My MPhil thesis applied quantitative methods to explore how cultural dimensions shape consumer decisions among Gen Z.

Beyond my own research, I have spent over four years delivering high-quality academic writing for students and researchers across disciplines — from media and communication studies to BBA, supply chain management, business studies, and social sciences.

I am currently preparing for PhD applications focusing on Gen Z stealth activism, coded resistance, and digital repression in South Asia — bringing the same rigour and curiosity to every piece of writing I produce.
